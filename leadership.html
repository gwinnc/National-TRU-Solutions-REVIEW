Mr. Joe Legare

Program Manager

Joe’s 32 years of experience includes leading large (~$200M/yr) DOE programs, including daily operations of N3B LLCC and 650 staff, managed TRU waste to be disposed at WIPP, worked under regulatory environment similar to WIPP’s (NMED regulators, EPA region 6) regarding NM Consent Order and interactions with NM stakeholders. He provided engineering support for WIPP ventilation during the 2014 incident recovery. Providing leadership with a sense of urgency – TRU waste shipments went from zero in FY18 to completing the first shipment within five months in his role as VP/Executive Officer; then shipments increased to 29 in FY21.

Mr. George Sturgis

Capital Asset Projects Manager

George brings 40 years of relevant experience in mining industry CAPs valued at $200M-$500M, including Hecla Mining’s $600M portfolio of 25 mining projects with an aggregate performance delivering under budget and on schedule using EVMS best practices. He has led 34 shaft projects totaling >96K vertical feet in many geology types in 7 countries. He is an internationally recognized SME in heavy civil surface/underground mining construction, including ventilation and hoisting system installation. Additionally, he has led shaft and lateral development of 15 shafts of similar design/depth as WIPP.

Ms. Kate Gregory

Integration Manager

Kate leads development of >$500M in multifaceted infrastructure recapitalization programs for DOE Fermilab; responsible for laboratory integration management, schedule and budget performance. She has managed an $11.4B annual budget ($8B construction programs, $3.4B services) – achieved annual fiscal targets within 0.01% of a $3.4B services fund. Kate has led development, integration, and implementation of multiple 3-5 year strategic infrastructure plans to align a 19,000 person, globally-dispersed organization.

Mr. Mark Krauss

WIPP Operations Manager

Mark has 34 years of relevant experience operating nuclear facilities for DOE/NNSS projects. He is currently the Senior MSTS Director, leading a 200-person team with a $240M/yr budget. Mark leads the NNSS U1a Complex Enhancements Project (UCEP), a $510 underground facility expansion, providing base infrastructure to accommodate installation of nuclear material subcritical experimentation equipment – a direct WIPP analog. He has been directly responsible for TRU waste management activities for seven years at NNSS. He has also assisted with WIPP restart supporting management self-assessment, contractor and DOE operational readiness reviews (ORR).

Mr. Vince Gomes

Mining/Underground Operations Manager

Vince brings 30 years of relevant experience. He currently leads mining/underground construction to support expansion of experiments critical to NNSS U1 a missions, coordinates 200 construction and laboratory personnel with a $40M/yr budget. Also, he is responsible for the coordination of underground infrastructure support and safety. Vince received a Performance Award for an 18-month special assignment supporting WIPP recovery as Acting Project Engineering Manager.

Mr. Stacey Alderson

Environment, Safety & Health Manager

Stacey has 25 years of experience leading ES&H and nuclear safety programs at DOE facilities. He currently manages the ES&H program for DOE's U1 a project, with up to 200 personnel underground and is responsible for U and P tunnels underground ES&H. Stacey leads 140-person ES&H staff with $20M/yr budget, including 13 managers, nine functional organizations and two occupational medicine clinics. In addition to that, he led the ES&H program to reduce recordable injuries by 75% over 22 months, resulting in DOE VPP's Star of Excellence award.
